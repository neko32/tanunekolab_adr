# 9. vsm

Date: 2026-04-26

## Status

Accepted

## Context

In our current software delivery lifecycle, we lack a quantitative understanding of the lead time from code commit to production release, as well as the "idle time" present in each phase.

Particularly in large-scale system updates involving multiple components, discussions regarding bottlenecks tend to be based on intuition rather than data. To address this, we need a framework to visualize the flow and enable continuous process improvement (Kaizen) based on objective metrics.

Related ideas to know:
- [Lean Principle](https://www.atlassian.com/ja/agile/project-management/lean-principles)

- [DevOps](https://www.atlassian.com/ja/agile/devops)

## Decision

We will adopt **Value Stream Mapping (VSM)** as a standard methodology to visualize the end-to-end software development lifecycle (SDLC).

### Implementation Details
**Mapping Scope**: From requirement definition to deployment, including the post-production feedback loop.

**Key Metrics to Measure:**

* **Lead Time (LT)**: Total time from the start to the end of the process.

* **Process Time (PT)**: The actual "touch time" spent working on the task.

* **Percent Complete and Accurate (%C&A)**: The percentage of work that can be passed to the next stage without requiring rework.

**Cadence**: Update the map quarterly, or upon significant organizational changes or tool migrations.

## Consequences

1. **Identification of Bottlenecks:** To avoid local optimization and focus on reducing "waiting time" that obstructs the overall flow.

2. **Shared Language:** To enable business stakeholders and engineering teams to discuss delivery delays based on the same dataset.

3. **ROI Clarity:** To quantify how much improvements in CI/CD pipelines or automated testing actually contribute to shortening the total lead time.

### Impact

#### Positive

- Improved delivery efficiency and reduction of non-value-added waiting time.
- Better visualization of inter-team dependencies.
- Reduced developer frustration caused by "hand-off" delays.

#### Negative

- Initial mapping workshops require several hours to days of effort.
- Overhead for setting up metrics collection tools (e.g., leveraging Jira or GitHub APIs).

### Alternatives Considered

- **DORA Metrics (Four Keys) only**:

    - **Comparison**: While DORA metrics are excellent for measuring "outcomes," VSM is superior for identifying specific inefficiencies within the process. We recommend using them in tandem.
